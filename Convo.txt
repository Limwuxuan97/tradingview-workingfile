## The 41-Month Cycle: What Is It and Where Does It Come From?
In his recent technical outlooks and podcast appearances, Newton frequently references a 41-month cycle (roughly 180 weeks or 3.5 years).
 * The Concept: This cycle suggests that the stock market experiences rhythmic peaks and troughs roughly every 41 months. Newton uses this composite model to project when the market’s underlying momentum will naturally expand or exhaust itself. Recently, this cycle suggested a market peak in mid-2025, paving the way for a weakening trend into 2026.
 * Historical Backing & Proof: The 41-month cycle was popularized by Edward R. Dewey, an economist who founded the Foundation for the Study of Cycles in 1941. Dewey dedicated his life to statistically proving that natural and economic phenomena follow predictable, recurring rhythms.
 * The Economic Reasoning: Tangibly, this cycle closely mirrors the Kitchin Cycle—a well-documented macroeconomic theory discovered in the 1920s by Joseph Kitchin. The Kitchin cycle is driven by business inventory dynamics. It takes about 40 to 41 months for companies to over-produce, realize they have too much inventory, cut back production, clear the excess, and then ramp up production again. This boom-and-bust inventory flow directly impacts corporate earnings and, consequently, stock market trends.
